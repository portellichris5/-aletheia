About Aletheia

Built for decisions distorted by persuasive claims and incomplete evidence.

Why Aletheia exists

Important commercial decisions are often made using fragmented information, persuasive marketing and assumptions that have never been made explicit. Aletheia organises that uncertainty into a transparent evidence record.

What makes it different

Each assessment distinguishes established facts from observations, assumptions, missing information and decision risk. It does not claim certainty where the evidence does not support it.

Where it is going

The longer-term objective is to progressively automate evidence gathering and draft assessment generation while retaining transparent reasoning and human review.

For decisions where getting it wrong is expensive.

Aletheia is intended for people considering software, AI products, coaching programmes, suppliers, proposals and other purchases where persuasive claims are easier to find than independent evidence.

The Aletheia method

A consistent assisted-assessment process designed to turn a difficult decision into a transparent and auditable evidence record.

1

Define the decision

Clarify what is being considered, the cost of error and the outcome that matters.

2

Examine the evidence

Review submitted materials and relevant public information.

3

Separate fact from assumption

Identify what is supported, inferred, asserted or absent.

4

Assess risk and contradiction

Expose inconsistencies, dependencies and unanswered questions.

5

State the decision position

Proceed, proceed with caution, seek more evidence, or do not proceed.
