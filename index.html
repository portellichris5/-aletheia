The Aletheia review process

A consistent review structure designed to turn a difficult decision into an auditable evidence record.

Define the decision.
Clarify exactly what is being considered and what outcome matters.
Examine the material.
Review submitted documents and relevant public evidence.
Separate fact from assumption.
Identify what is known, inferred, claimed or absent.
Assess risk and confidence.
Explain where uncertainty remains and why it matters.
Deliver a written review.
Provide findings, questions and a clear decision position.
