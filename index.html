The Aletheia method

A consistent assisted-review process designed to turn a difficult decision into a transparent and auditable evidence record.

1

Define the decision

Clarify what is being considered, the cost of error and the outcome that matters.

2

Examine the evidence

Review submitted materials and relevant public information.

3

Separate fact from assumption

Identify what is supported, inferred, asserted or absent.

4

Assess risk and contradiction

Expose inconsistencies, dependencies and unanswered questions.

5

State the decision position

Proceed, proceed with caution, seek more evidence, or do not proceed.
